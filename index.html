    Adopt a Native Elder Website Redesign

    Project Summary
    User Research
    Current Stie Analysist
    User Experience
    Look & Feel
    Feedback, Reactions & Next Steps

    PROBLEM

    Adopt a Native Elders mission is to bring awareness and aid to the Navajo nation through the means of donation of funds, items, volunteering and the selling of authentic Navajo goods. The current webpage feels outdated, unprofessional and has insecure payment options

    SOLUTION

    Review and analysis of the current website, the needs of users as well as a redesign of the information architecture, navigation and providing a professional and more modernized 'face-lift'. We know we have succeeded in our goals when our users and stakeholders are able to intuitively navigate, safely donate with confidence, and the organization sees an increase those interactions and donations

    WHO IS OUR USER?

    Looking into the proto persona, this is someone who has the time and money to become involved in a cause or organization. This can often mean this is someone older. Keeping this front of mind affects some of the decision making throughout the redesign is crucial. This person also may have a connection to Native American communities, or they may not and are just looking to get involved.
    “I love what is offered in the shop, but feel uneasy about the payment page”
    “I want to be more involved in this community, but find it hard to navigate the website to learn all that they do.”

    USER INTERVIEWS & SURVEYS

    Surveys gathering information on how people prefer to give back to a community and interact with non profit websites through google forms. 23 survey responses gathered and 5 user interviews.
    Main comments and takeaways from the user survey
    -People want to give back in many ways, not just through monetary donations. Organizations that offer multiple types of interaction appeal to a wider audience
    -Word of mouth and internet equally inform people about an organization
    -Secure credit or debit payment methods are crucial to a users decision to donate.

    CURRENT SITE ANALYSIS

    A deep dive into the current Adopt a Native Elder website rating the aesthetics, content, navigation and efficiency. Very few categories rated above a 2 showing a clear path for improvement.
    Through card sorting the pages, links and paths we can visualize all avenues that a user can take on the current site. This provides insight to see how to consolodate, focus on key aspects of the site and our call to actions

    USER TESTING

    5 user tests were performed on the current webpage to gauge user ability to navigate, perform tasks and get their feelings and impressions on the site.
    Main comments and takeaways from the user tests
    -Overwhelmed with options on the navigation
    -Donate now call to action feels like an ad rather than a part of the website
    -Donation and payment collection felt like a scam rather than a reputable site
    -Color scheme not pleasing
    -mages are impactful as they are of the actual people who are a part of the organization

    LOOK & FEEL

    WIREFRAMES
    Crucial to the redesign for are the homepage and the donation page. The homepage is where our users will find all the information to gauge whether or not they want to give to the cause.
    Designing the page with a funnel to draw the user down and keep them engaged throughout the site. The design is made to be intuitive based on the user demographic being older and may not be as tech savvy. This will help to raise interaction and reduce frustration.
    The donation process was decided to be a module rather than a separate page for a quick and easy user experience

    STYLE TILE
    The original logo from Adopt a Native Elder was left unchanged as it has significance with the organization as well as cultural iconography. The style tile was design to work with it.
    Inspiration was pulled from the Navajo nation as well as from items that are sold in the website shop. The idea was to enhance what already exists rather than erase and replace.
    It was important to use real life imagery from the organization. Showing the actual people who are impacted by Adopt a Native Elder as well as the volunteer events creates more of a connection with the user. Create that bond between the user and actual people in the organization rather than with stock images.
    HIGH-FIDELITY
    There is clear contrast between the existing donation path and the new donation module. A pop up module for the donation creates a faster, more enjoyable experience for the user. The design is professional and adding “Secure Donation” adds trust for the user
    Personal touches such as how to say Hello in the Navajo language to create more of a connection between Adopt and Native Elder and the user.

    FEEDBACK, REACTIONS & NEXT STEPS

    Overall user testing with the redesign was received very well and usability and satisfaction being much higher. Many of the testing subjects were used in the initial phase to test the current website, and then to test the redesign. Some feedback given was used in high fidelity iterations or slated for phase two of the project. Things like more use of iconography or images of the types of payments that can be used for the donation module.
    Listening to our User Feedback after our design was finalized has been extremely gratifying. We love that we have succeeded in so many ways to bring Adopt A Native Elder’s web presence a much needed ‘tune-up’. Adopt A Native Elder has 3 major tasks for Users: Donate, Volunteer, and Shop. Moving into our next phase of design, using our new theme and user-centric methodology, the Volunteer and Shop pages could greatly benefit.
